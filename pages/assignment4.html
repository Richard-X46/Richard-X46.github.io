Assignment 4: The AI Divide in the GTA

A Data Scrollytelling Experience

This project explores the growing economic divide created by AI adoption in the Greater Toronto Area. Through interactive visualizations and narrative storytelling, we follow two personas navigating the AI economy: Jiya Sharma, an AI engineer thriving in tech, and Chris Anderson, a logistics supervisor facing automation-driven job insecurity.

Key themes explored:

  • Wage gap trends between AI/tech and traditional industries
  • Education enrollment shifts toward AI and computer science
  • AI exposure and complementarity across occupations
  • Accessible training pathways for workers in at-risk sectors
  • Geographic distribution of tech vs. business programs in Canada

Built with Streamlit, Plotly, Folium, and Pandas. Data sources include Statistics Canada, AI occupational exposure indices, and provincial enrollment records.

Meet the Personas

Jiya Sharma

Role: AI Engineer

Education: MSc in Computer Science

Situation: Secure career with growing opportunities in the AI sector. Benefits from high complementarity with AI tools.

Chris Anderson

Role: Logistics Supervisor

Education: Business Management

Situation: Facing job insecurity as automation replaces manual coordination. Exploring upskilling pathways to remain relevant.

Story Boards & Visualizations

The scrollytelling narrative is structured around key data insights visualized through interactive charts and maps:

Geographic distribution of AI/Tech vs Business enrollment across Canada

Tech vs Logistics: Wage gap over time

Education enrollment trends: Math/CS vs Business

AI complementarity vs exposure by occupation

Upskilling pathways: Cost and duration comparison

Interactive Folium map: Provincial enrollment patterns
